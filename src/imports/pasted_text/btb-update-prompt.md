## BTB Capital Website (Option B) — Client Feedback Round 1

---

## WHAT THE CLIENT SAID

The client loves the first cut but noted that BTB doesn't only manage the lending/investor side — borrowers and brokers are also their clients. The site needs to reflect that BTB serves all three audiences while keeping the service-led positioning (not reverting to the fund-manager structure of Option A).

**Reference site:** [njcapital.com.au](https://njcapital.com.au/) — George's previous business. NJ Capital handled this by placing "For the Investor" and "For the Borrower" sections side by side on the same page, rather than giving each audience a separate page. This kept the site feeling unified and service-led.

---

## WHAT NOT TO CHANGE

- **Don't add separate pages** for Borrowers or Brokers. The NJ Capital model (sections on the same page) is the right approach for a 3-person firm. Separate pages would pull the site back toward the Equity One structure.
- **Don't change the How It Works page.** The 8-step investor journey is the centrepiece and should remain investor-focused. Borrowers don't need to see that process — they need to know BTB is fast, flexible, and secured.
- **Don't change the brand styling.** All colours, typography, and logo usage stay per the brand guidelines already applied.
- **Don't change the overall tone.** The site should still feel like a trusted advisor, not a product catalogue. The borrower and broker sections should match the same conversational, switched-on tone as the rest of the site.
